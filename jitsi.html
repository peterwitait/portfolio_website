context


The first wave of the corona pandemic brought about new challeneges in daily life, and many took to technology in new ways to be together apart. As a result, as part of my degree in UX design I was challenged with redesigning Jitsi, a video conferencing platform to meet the needs of new and evolving digital subculture.

I chose to remodel Jitsi into a digital space for hosting and participating in online clubnights.

research


Research was carried out remotely through desk research, interviews with online partiers and DJs in the UK, Netherlands and US, observations and active participation in online clubnights and raves. I wanted to find out:

- how online parties organised the interactive experience digitally
- how the interface compensated for the lack of physical closeness
- what drives users to participate in these events

After conducting my research I analysed my data and came to the following key insights to consider:

- users relish variety and expect the unexpected
- users get off on entertaining others and making them feel connected
- users are thick skinned but cannot stand intolerance

I compiled my findings into a user persona to help guide the ideation process.

prototyping

Once the users’ needs were analysed I ideated upon them using various diverging methods such as sketching and brainstorming. I also invited some of my interviewees to take part in a digital brainwriting and collage making excercise to gain a fuller understanding of what the final product should be.

Then I converged my findings down into defined points through further analysis and clustering, before creating a simple interactive wireframe prototype. to be tested on potential users.

result

The final result is a platform which aims to mimic the real life interactions and spaces found in a physical club digitally. Users are free to roam different rooms and chat with the constant flow of fellow partiers that float along their screen. They can interact with each other on different levels, from a wink to then offering a drink, and from chatting on the dancefloor to one-on-one conversations at their ‘table’.

Users are also able to express themselves and entertain others through a choice of dynamic backgrounds and effects that display within their window.

Security was one of the main users needs and this was satisfied by implementing a three level distancing system to block, ignore or report users for unacceptable behaviour. This is available to any user from any page within a few clicks, providing that they can see the user or are interacting with them.
